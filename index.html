What is crochet?

Crochet is the practice of crafting plushies, clothes, or small objects by making up yarn into a textured fabric thanks to a hook needle. It is a handicraft art.

Though it originally is a more old kind of hobbby, and right now you might picture your grandma doing it, crochet actually became trending during the pandemic back in 2020. It makes sense because it asks a lot of time and patience, especially when you're learning, but also creativity and imagination.

Crochet vs Knitting

Before you say it, crochet is NOT the same as knitting! Knitting requires two sticks while with crochet you hold the hook in one hand, and your project in the other one. Basically, knitting is more adapted to clothe-making as it uses less yarn than crochet for the same surface; and crochet includes a wider variety of techniques which is more interesting for details on plushies, decorations, etc.
